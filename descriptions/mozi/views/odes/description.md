Despite his vicious disapproval of Confucius, Mozi places great authority in the same classical documents, such as the *Odes*. Mozi appeals to the *Odes* at one point to argue that ghosts and spirits are real. Because of this common heritage, by the time of the unification and the Han dynasty, Confucius and Mozi were often placed in the same category: in "Faults of Qin," Ji Yi writes that the common man who overthrew the Qin empire had "neither the worth of Confucius and Mo Di," and Liu An lumped their followers into the "civil" category of thinkers in the *Huinanzi*.
