Xunzi writes that Huizi's focus on words did not comprehend the importance of actual things, and that he and Gongsun Longzi created chaos in their abuse of language. In "Chapter Twenty-Two: On Correct Naming," Xunzi discusses at length the importance of clear and simple names for effective communication social harmony. To him, the thinkers of the School of Names made various mistakes that took them far from reality (giving as examples Hui Shi and Gongsun Longzi's paradoxes), and understanding these pitfalls will prevent their confusion from arising again.
