Han Feizi describes Shen Buhai's administrative methods (*shu*) as the counterpart to Lord Shang's (*fa*). Without one or the other, a ruler will fail. The administrative methods keep the ministers accountable and ensure that they accomplish what they put forward to the ruler. However, without a strong and unified law, this accountability will merely be symbolic and the ministers will still be able to get around it. A strong and unified law succeeds in incentivizing and disincentivizing the people, but ministers will be able to divert the strength of the state to their own interests.
